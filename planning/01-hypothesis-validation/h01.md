# H1 — Boundary under-classification vs. realized outcomes
**Group:** Problem existence (H1–H4) · **Tests assumption:** A1 (with A2 as prerequisite) · **Sub-problem:** P1

## Hypothesis statement (source §5)
Customers near segment decision boundaries are systematically under-classified relative to realized outcomes.

## Falsification condition (verbatim)
> "Boundary cohorts' realized risk matches assigned segment"

## Validation method (source, verbatim)
Backtest: realized default rates of boundary cohorts vs. their assigned segment's expected PD band.

## Required data (→ `../03-data/data-inventory-tracker.md`)
| Item | Use |
|---|---|
| M1 Decision engine outputs (scores, segments, timestamps, model version) — historical | Identify score positions relative to cut-offs; build boundary cohorts |
| M2 Segment cut-off definitions and change history | Define "near boundary" correctly per period; avoid mixing cut-off regimes |
| M4 Credit performance outcomes (24–36 months) | Realized default/delinquency — the ground-truth side of the backtest |
| M7 Risk appetite statement, credit policy, policy ranges per segment | The segment's expected PD band the cohort is compared against |
| M3 Offer data (context) | Interpret what under-classification cost in offer terms (feeds D1 narrative) |

## Analysis protocol (business/analytical terms — no model building)
1. **Prerequisite:** ground-truth convention signed by Risk (A2 → E2). Without it this analysis is not accepted (E2 consequence).
2. **Cohort definition (to pre-register with Risk):** customers whose score at assignment fell within an agreed distance of a segment cut-off (distance convention set with Risk, e.g., a score-band width per boundary), split by segment pair (upper boundary of segment n vs. lower boundary of segment n+1), by origination vintage, and by cut-off regime (per M2 history).
3. **Comparison logic:** for each boundary cohort, compare realized default/delinquency over the agreed outcome window against the assigned segment's expected PD band (M7). A cohort whose realized risk sits materially below its assigned band — and within or below the band of the better adjacent segment — is evidence of under-classification.
4. **Success/materiality thresholds:** the "materially below band" margin and the affected-population share that counts as "systematic and material" (E1's agreed threshold, OQ-13) are set with Risk **before** results are known.
5. **Controls and caveats:** stratify by product (OQ-18); document the selective-labels problem verbatim as a permanent caveat (§6.2, §6.5 R-DAT-05): outcomes exist only for credit actually granted at actually offered levels; OPT3 macro overlays may contextualize outcome periods.
