# H3 — Thin-file and new-to-bank customers start too low and migrate up
**Group:** Problem existence (H1–H4) · **Tests assumption:** A1 (data-limitations root cause, §1.2) · **Sub-problem:** P1

## Hypothesis statement (source §5)
Thin-file and new-to-bank customers are disproportionately placed in lower segments and disproportionately migrate upward later.

## Falsification condition (verbatim)
> "Thin-file customers' initial segments prove accurate"

## Validation method (source, verbatim)
Cohort analysis by data-richness at origination.

## Required data
| Item | Use |
|---|---|
| M1 Decision engine outputs — historical | Initial segment at origination; score inputs' completeness where recorded |
| M5 Segment migration history | Upward-migration trajectories after origination |
| M4 Credit performance outcomes | Whether early segments were *accurate* (the falsification test) vs. merely sticky |
| U6 Bureau snapshots and refresh dates | Data-richness indicator: bureau file depth at origination |

**Data note:** a "data-richness at origination" flag may not exist as a field; it may need to be derived from M1 input-completeness or U6 file depth. If neither supports it, log the gap in the tracker (blocking issue) and record H3 as "scheduled" rather than tested — do not proxy it with an unagreed definition. Definition of "thin-file" and "new-to-bank" is agreed with Risk at protocol pre-registration.

## Analysis protocol (business/analytical terms)
1. **Cohort definition:** origination cohorts split by data-richness tier (thin-file / new-to-bank / full-file — definitions signed by Risk) within product and vintage.
2. **Comparison logic (two parts):**
   a. *Placement:* initial segment distribution by data-richness tier vs. full-file peers with comparable realized outcomes.
   b. *Trajectory:* subsequent upward-migration rates and time-to-upgrade by tier; then the accuracy test — did thin-file customers' **realized outcomes** (M4) match their initial segment's band (accurate = falsified) or the band they later migrated to (supports H3)?
3. **Thresholds:** the "disproportionate" margin is pre-registered with Risk.
4. **Caveats:** selective labels (only granted credit observable); survivorship in migration data (closed accounts leave the panel — document per R-DAT-05).
