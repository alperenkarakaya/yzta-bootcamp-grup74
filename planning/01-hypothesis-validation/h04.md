# H4 — Manual overrides are asymmetric and poorly calibrated downward
**Group:** Problem existence (H1–H4) · **Tests assumption:** A1 (override-behavior root cause, §1.2) · **Sub-problem:** P1

## Hypothesis statement (source §5)
Manual overrides are asymmetric (downgrades ≫ upgrades) and downgrades show worse calibration than the model itself.

## Falsification condition (verbatim)
> "Overrides are symmetric or add accuracy"

## Validation method (source, verbatim)
Override log analysis with outcome tracking.

## Required data
| Item | Use |
|---|---|
| M6 Manual override logs with reasons and outcomes | The override events, direction, stated reasons, authority level |
| M1 Decision engine outputs | The pre-override model segment (the counterfactual assignment) |
| M4 Credit performance outcomes | Realized outcomes of overridden vs. non-overridden comparable customers |

## Analysis protocol (business/analytical terms)
1. **Asymmetry test:** share of overrides that are downgrades vs. upgrades, by product, channel, authority level, and period. (Week-1 quick answer expected from OQ-04; this analysis verifies it from M6.)
2. **Calibration test:** for overridden customers, compare realized outcomes (M4) against (a) the band of the segment the model assigned and (b) the band of the segment the override placed them in. Downgrades whose realized risk matches the *model's* segment better than the override's segment indicate the override subtracted accuracy.
3. **Reason coding:** classify override reasons (from M6 free text/codes) into a taxonomy agreed with Risk — evidence for the §1.2 "asymmetric error costs" and "override behavior" root causes, and for interview triangulation (Credit Risk, Front line guides).
4. **Thresholds:** the asymmetry ratio and calibration margin that count as "systematic" are pre-registered with Risk.
5. **Caveats:** override logs may be incomplete or reasons unrecorded (Internal Audit interview probes this — decision lineage); selective labels caveat applies.
