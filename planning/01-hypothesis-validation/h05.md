# H5 — Within-segment signals separate materially different risk levels
**Group:** Value mechanisms (H5–H8) · **Tests assumption:** A7 (signals exist and are usable) · **Sub-problem:** P2

## Hypothesis statement (source §5)
Within-segment behavioral signals (e.g., transaction stability, deposit trends) can distinguish materially different risk levels inside a single segment.

## Falsification condition (verbatim)
> "Within-segment outcomes are homogeneous / signals add no separation"

## Validation method (source, verbatim)
Retrospective analysis: within-segment outcome variance explained by candidate signals *(analysis of signal existence only — no model building)*.

**Scope guard:** the italicized source constraint is binding. This analysis establishes whether separation *exists*; it does not build, select, or recommend any model, scoring logic, or signal weighting. Any output that reads like a feature list for a model is out of Phase 1 scope.

## Required data
| Item | Use |
|---|---|
| U1 Transaction/behavioral banking data (inflows, balances, payment behavior) | Candidate signal families |
| M1 Decision engine outputs | Segment membership defining the within-segment populations |
| M4 Credit performance outcomes | Outcome variance to be examined within each segment |

## Analysis protocol (business/analytical terms)
1. **Legal-basis precondition:** U1 was collected for servicing; its use here needs a confirmed legal basis (purpose limitation — R-REG-03, R-DAT-03). Data Office + Compliance disposition in the tracker gates this analysis.
2. **Population:** customers of a given segment (start with the largest and the most boundary-dense segments per H1 outputs), observed over the agreed outcome window.
3. **Signal families (business-defined, agreed with Risk and Compliance):** e.g., income-inflow stability, balance trends, payment punctuality — screened by Compliance for proxy risk (R-AI-03 bias amplification) *before* analysis.
4. **Comparison logic:** within each segment, group customers by simple, transparent banded values of each candidate signal family and compare realized outcome rates across bands. Material, consistent outcome spread across bands (margin pre-registered with Risk) demonstrates separation exists; flat outcomes across bands falsify.
5. **Fairness guard:** any signal family showing separation is flagged to the H14 workstream to check correlation with protected/proxy attributes before it appears in any opportunity narrative.
6. **Caveats:** selective labels; behavioral data quality (R-DAT-02) — quality profiling precedes analysis.
