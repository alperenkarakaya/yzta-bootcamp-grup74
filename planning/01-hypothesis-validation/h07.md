# H7 — Past within-policy limit increases lifted utilization without proportional delinquency
**Group:** Value mechanisms (H5–H8) · **Tests assumption:** A10 (win-win is real, per segment) · **Sub-problem:** P3 (and O1/O6 mechanism)

## Hypothesis statement (source §5)
Customers whose limits are increased within policy show utilization uplift without proportional delinquency increase (based on the bank's own historical limit-increase programs).

## Falsification condition (verbatim)
> "Past increases produced delinquency growth ≥ revenue growth"

## Validation method (source, verbatim)
Analysis of past proactive limit-increase campaigns.

**Why this matters:** the source calls U3 the "strongest available natural experiment" — the closest Phase 1 gets to causal evidence, given the counterfactual problem (§2.4: data on "what would have happened with a better offer" does not exist).

## Required data
| Item | Use |
|---|---|
| U3 Historical limit-increase campaign results | The natural experiment: who was increased, when, by how much |
| U2 Credit utilization and limit-usage patterns | Utilization response of increased customers |
| M4 Credit performance outcomes | Delinquency response over the post-campaign window |

## Analysis protocol (business/analytical terms)
1. **Campaign census first (OQ-03):** which campaigns ran, selection rules, volumes, outcomes as reported at the time — from Business's own post-mortems before touching row-level data.
2. **Cohort definition:** campaign-increased customers vs. a comparison group agreed with Risk (e.g., eligible-but-not-selected customers where selection rules allow a fair comparison; where they don't, document the comparability limits honestly rather than forcing a comparison).
3. **Comparison logic:** utilization uplift and revenue proxy vs. delinquency movement over matched windows; expressed risk-adjusted (consistent with the H6 method). Falsified if delinquency growth ≥ revenue growth.
4. **Segment cut:** results per segment (A10 discipline — win-win proven per segment, not asserted).
5. **Caveats:** campaign selection bias (campaigns targeted customers the bank already liked) — bounds how far results generalize; state this in D4 and D5 verbatim as a claim limit.
