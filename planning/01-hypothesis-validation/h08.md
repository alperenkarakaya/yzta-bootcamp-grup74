# H8 — Earlier re-scoring alone would move a meaningful population upward
**Group:** Value mechanisms (H5–H8) · **Tests assumption:** A1/A8 context · **Sub-problem:** P1 (stale subset) — O3 mechanism

## Hypothesis statement (source §5)
Earlier re-scoring of improving customers would move a meaningful population upward using the existing model alone.

## Falsification condition (verbatim)
> "Re-scoring frequency changes segment for a negligible population"

## Validation method (source, verbatim)
Simulate re-scoring cadence change on historical data.

**Scope guard:** "simulate" means replaying the bank's **own historical scores/segments** under an alternative review cadence — or, if stored score history is too sparse, having the **bank's model owner** re-run the bank's existing model on historical snapshots (OQ-25). No new scoring logic is built or proposed; this tests a scheduling change only (O3: "no new decision logic, only scheduling").

## Required data
| Item | Use |
|---|---|
| M1 Decision engine outputs — historical (all score events, timestamps, versions) | The score timeline to replay at a different cadence |
| M5 Segment migration history | Observed migrations under the current cadence (the baseline) |
| U6 Bureau snapshots and refresh dates | Whether earlier re-scores would have had fresher inputs available |

## Analysis protocol (business/analytical terms)
1. **Feasibility fork (OQ-25, resolve week 4):**
   - *Replay path:* where customers have multiple stored score events, measure how much earlier the migration-triggering score change was observable than the cadence acted on it.
   - *Re-run path:* bank model owner re-runs its own model on historical snapshots at hypothetical earlier dates; the engagement only analyzes the outputs.
2. **Cohort definition:** customers who migrated upward under the current cadence (from M5), plus a screen for "improving" customers whose next scheduled score was far away at the time of improvement.
3. **Comparison logic:** population share whose segment would have improved ≥1 segment earlier under cadence alternatives (agreed set, e.g., quarterly vs. annual — the alternatives are business scenarios, not design choices), and the average time-in-wrong-segment eliminated.
4. **Materiality threshold:** the "meaningful population" bar is pre-registered with Risk (consistent with the E1 threshold family, OQ-13).
5. **Caveats:** model version changes over the window (M1 version field) must be respected — do not mix scores across versions without Risk's guidance.
