# H9 — Dissatisfaction is driven by missing explanation and path, not the amount
**Group:** Customer (H9–H11) · **Tests assumption:** A3 · **Sub-problem:** customer-side materiality; O4 mechanism

## Hypothesis statement (source §5)
Offer-related dissatisfaction is driven more by lack of explanation and path-to-improvement than by the absolute limit.

## Falsification condition (verbatim)
> "Dissatisfaction verbatims are dominated by amount, indifferent to explanation"

## Validation method (source, verbatim)
Complaint verbatim analysis; small-sample customer interviews.

## Required data
| Item | Use |
|---|---|
| M8 Complaint data tagged to credit limits/offers | The verbatim corpus; complaint volumes as materiality evidence |
| U5 NPS / CSAT with credit-journey linkage | Quantitative dissatisfaction signal; baseline for D9 |

Plus: customer interviews per `../02-stakeholders/guide-customer.md` (governed research, consent per OQ-20).

## Analysis protocol (business/analytical terms)
1. **Complaint taxonomy (weeks 3–4):** classify limit/offer-tagged complaints (M8) into agreed themes — amount, explanation/opacity, process speed, fairness perception, path-to-improvement absence. Taxonomy agreed with Customer Service/Front line (they know the corpus) and Compliance (regulatory complaint categories). This taxonomy is also the baseline for the §8.2 complaint-rate KPI.
2. **Comparison logic:** theme distribution — H9 supported if explanation/path themes materially outweigh pure-amount themes (margin agreed before coding); falsified if amount dominates and explanation is indifferent.
3. **Customer interviews (weeks 5–6):** 8–12 customers across near-boundary / thin-file / complained cohorts; semi-structured per the customer guide; coded against the same taxonomy for triangulation.
4. **Cross-check:** NPS verbatims (U5) coded with the same theme set.
5. **Caveats:** complaints are a self-selected sample (customers who bothered to complain); interviews are small-sample qualitative — claims stated as directional, not statistical.

## Stakeholder sign-off
- Taxonomy: Customer Service + Compliance. Research protocol: Compliance (consent). Results: D4 (Risk, for rigor of claims), D1 (customer-side materiality), D9 (baselines).

## Estimated effort
1.5 analyst-weeks (taxonomy + coding) + interview program (shared with H10). Elapsed weeks 3–6.

## Feeds
- **Deliverables:** D1, D4, D5 (O4 case — explanation layer "addresses satisfaction directly even where the offer is correct"), D9 (NPS/complaint baselines).
- **Exit criteria:** **E3** (O4 value rationale), **E7** (D9 baselines).
- If falsified (amount dominates): A3 is falsified, O4 weakens, and the value case must rest on offer economics (H6/H7) — a legitimate pivot signal.
