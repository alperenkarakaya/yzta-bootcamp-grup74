# H10 — Explainability increases acceptance and trust
**Group:** Customer (H9–H11) · **Tests assumption:** A3/A9 (customer & staff trust side) · **Sub-problem:** O4 mechanism; trust precondition

## Hypothesis statement (source §5)
Explainability of offer rationale increases customer acceptance and trust in AI-assisted recommendations.

## Falsification condition (verbatim)
> "Explanations do not change acceptance/trust indicators"

## Validation method (source, verbatim)
Qualitative testing of explanation concepts with customers and front-line staff.

**Scope guard:** "explanation concepts" are wording/structure stimuli for research (e.g., offer rationale phrased with vs. without a path-to-improvement) — not product designs. No UI, no generation logic, no system behavior is designed in Phase 1.

## Required data
| Item | Use |
|---|---|
| U5 NPS / CSAT with credit-journey linkage | Baseline trust/acceptance indicators (D9: "trust measures piloted qualitatively in Phase 1") |

Plus: qualitative sessions — customers (shared recruitment with H9, per customer guide) and front-line staff (per front-line guide).

## Analysis protocol (business/analytical terms)
1. **Concept set:** 2–3 explanation concepts varying explanation depth and path-to-improvement presence, written in plain language with Compliance review (no promises of better offers; no misleading implications — R-REG-05 explainability-rights context).
2. **Customer sessions:** within the H9 interview sample, present concepts and capture comparative reactions: comprehension, perceived fairness, stated trust in an "AI-assisted" framing (tests the §3 executive concern about "AI decides your credit" perception at customer level).
3. **Staff sessions:** front-line staff walk through explaining a two-layer outcome using each concept (their §3 concern: "the AI adjusted it" makes explanations harder — R-OPS-03). Capture whether concepts make explanations easier or harder at first contact.
4. **Indicators:** simple pre-agreed qualitative indicators (comprehension / fairness perception / trust direction / staff explainability) — these become the D9 "explainability/trust indicators" baseline.
5. **Falsification reading:** if concepts do not move acceptance/trust indicators in either audience, H10 is falsified regardless of how much participants "liked" the materials.
