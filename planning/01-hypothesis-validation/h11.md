# H11 — Under-classified customers attrite to competitors within 12 months
**Group:** Customer (H9–H11) · **Tests assumption:** A1's cost side; competitive impact (§1.3) · **Sub-problem:** value of retention (all P's)

## Hypothesis statement (source §5)
Under-classified customers have measurably higher attrition to competitors within 12 months.

## Falsification condition (verbatim)
> "Attrition is uncorrelated with boundary status"

## Validation method (source, verbatim)
Attrition analysis by segment-boundary cohort.

## Required data
| Item | Use |
|---|---|
| U4 Attrition/closure data with destination indicators where known | Attrition events; competitor-destination signal where recorded |
| M1 Decision engine outputs + M2 cut-off definitions | Boundary-cohort construction (same convention as H1 — reuse, don't redefine) |
| OPT2 Competitor offer intelligence (optional) | Context only: whether competitor offers to this profile are plausibly better (source purpose: "Competitive context for attrition hypothesis") |

## Analysis protocol (business/analytical terms)
1. **Cohort reuse:** boundary cohorts defined identically to H1 (single pre-registered convention — one definition across the phase, or results won't compose in D1).
2. **Comparison logic:** 12-month attrition/closure rates of near-boundary cohorts vs. mid-segment peers of the same segment, by product and vintage. Falsified if attrition shows no correlation with boundary status.
3. **Destination readout:** where U4 carries destination indicators, report share leaving to competitors; where absent, state plainly that destination is unobserved (do not infer).
4. **Value linkage:** attrition deltas convert to retention value inside the H6/D5 risk-adjusted method (not as a separate gross number).
5. **Caveats:** attrition has many drivers; correlation with boundary status is directional evidence, not attribution. OPT2, if available, is context — never treated as causal evidence.
