# H12 — Risk and Model Governance will accept a bounded, auditable layer
**Group:** Organizational/regulatory (H12–H14) · **Tests assumption:** A6, A9 · **Sub-problem:** organizational readiness (Question 3 of the charter)

## Hypothesis statement (source §5)
Risk and Model Governance will accept an offer-optimization layer if it is bounded within existing policy ranges and fully auditable.

## Falsification condition (verbatim)
> "Governance signals that any offer-influencing logic requires full credit-model treatment regardless of bounds"

## Validation method (source, verbatim)
Structured governance interviews; mock governance review of the concept.

## Required data
No §7 analytical data. Inputs: M7 (risk appetite statement, credit policy — the bounds the concept claims to respect), the bank's model-risk policy (model definition criteria), and precedent classifications of existing overlays (pricing engines, campaign tools) gathered in the Model Risk interview.

## Validation protocol (business terms)
1. **Structured interviews (weeks 3–4):** Credit Risk and Model Risk/Validation per their interview guides — specifically: what makes something a "model" under the bank's model-risk policy; how existing non-model overlays are classified; what bounds/audit properties would keep an offer layer in "advisory/process" territory.
2. **Mock governance review (weeks 4–6):** present the *concept* (bridge layer bounded by the §4 "shall never" list, within-policy-range operation, full audit trail) to a mock panel of Model Risk + Risk + Internal Audit, run per the bank's real review format. The artifact reviewed is the concept description and boundary statement — no design.
3. **Acceptance evidence:** documented panel positions — accept-in-principle / accept-with-conditions (capture the conditions verbatim; they become D10 requirements) / reject-regardless-of-bounds (falsification).
4. **Triangulation:** cross-check interview positions against the H13 legal view (internal governance and external classification can diverge; both must pass — E4 and E8 are separate gates).

## Stakeholder sign-off
- Mock review format: Model Risk. Outcome record: Model Risk + Internal Audit confirm the write-up reflects their position (their E8 acceptance of D10 is the formal act; this validates it's achievable).

## Estimated effort
0.5 analyst-weeks + interview series + one half-day mock review. Elapsed weeks 3–6.

## Feeds
- **Deliverables:** D3 (positions, tensions), D10 (conditions → governance proposal), D4 (disposition).
- **Exit criteria:** **E8** (governance model accepted — hard gate).
- If falsified: the concept as framed cannot proceed without full credit-model treatment — a scope/positioning pivot (or stop) goes to the steering committee. No-go is a valid outcome.
