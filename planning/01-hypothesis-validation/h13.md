# H13 — Regulatory classification can remain "offer optimization within approved policy"
**Group:** Organizational/regulatory (H12–H14) · **Tests assumption:** A6 · **Sub-problem:** regulatory viability (Question 2 of the charter)

## Hypothesis statement (source §5)
The regulatory classification of the bridge layer can remain "offer optimization within approved policy" rather than "credit decisioning," given the jurisdiction's rules.

## Falsification condition (verbatim)
> "Legal opinion concludes the layer is a credit decision model"

## Validation method (source, verbatim)
Legal/Compliance opinion; precedent review; (if available) informal regulator dialogue.

## Required data
No §7 analytical data. Inputs: the concept boundary statement (charter §2 "shall never" list), M7 policy documents (what "within approved policy" concretely means), engine vendor contracts (via D2 discovery — interception/augmentation clauses, OQ-07), jurisdiction set (OQ-17), and precedent classifications (internal and, where public, supervisory).

## Validation protocol (process level — detailed in `../05-risk-and-compliance/regulatory-workplan.md`)
1. **Terms of reference (week 1):** Compliance/Legal commissioned to opine on: (a) classification of a layer that operates strictly inside the §4 boundaries, per opportunity type O1–O8; (b) which opportunities survive under which classification; (c) conditions that would flip the classification.
2. **Jurisdiction scan (weeks 2–4):** consumer-credit rules, model-governance expectations, data-protection/purpose-limitation constraints, explainability rights, AI-specific regulation (e.g., the source's §6.3 note that the EU AI Act treats creditworthiness AI as high-risk — applicability depends on OQ-17).
3. **Precedent review:** how comparable overlays (pricing engines, campaign eligibility tools, pre-approval logic) are classified internally and by the supervisor.
4. **Informal regulator dialogue (optional, per source "(if available)"):** only via Compliance, per `../02-stakeholders/guide-regulator-dialogue.md` — temperature-check, never a submission.
5. **Output:** written opinion (the E4 test is "Written legal/compliance opinion (D7) confirms at least one opportunity (O1–O8) can proceed under an acceptable classification") with per-opportunity viability flags consumed by D5.

## Stakeholder sign-off
- Opinion issued by Compliance/Legal; **signed by the Chief Compliance Officer** (D7 sign-off).

## Estimated effort
0.5 analyst-weeks coordination; 2–4 weeks elapsed counsel time (external counsel budget TBD by CL). Elapsed weeks 1–8 (ToR wk1 → draft wk6 → signed wk8).

## Feeds
- **Deliverables:** D7 (core), D5 (viability flags), D4 (disposition), D11 (conditions carried into Mid Phase charter).
- **Exit criteria:** **E4** (regulatory path viable).
- If falsified: E4's consequence applies — "Restrict scope to compliant opportunities or stop." O5 (analytics only) and O4/O7 (no offer-influencing automation) are the designed fallback scope (§6.3 mitigation: "design opportunities O1–O5 to stay within approved policy ranges").
