# H14 — Under-classification does not correlate with protected attributes (or correcting it reduces disparity)
**Group:** Organizational/regulatory (H12–H14) · **Tests assumption:** fairness position (§1.3 regulator lens; §6.2 bias amplification) · **Sub-problem:** fairness exposure across P1–P3

## Hypothesis statement (source §5)
Under-classification does not correlate with protected attributes — or if it does, correcting it *reduces* disparate impact (making the layer a fairness improvement, not a risk).

## Falsification condition (verbatim)
> "Correction would widen disparity, or the audit itself is legally blocked"

## Validation method (source, verbatim)
Fairness audit of current segment assignments vs. available protected/proxy attributes.

**Design intent (source §5 note, preserved):** if current under-classification disproportionately affects protected groups, the bridge layer flips from "compliance risk" to "compliance remediation" — a decisive argument in governance, worth testing early.

## Required data
| Item | Use |
|---|---|
| M1 Decision engine outputs | Current segment assignments to audit |
| M4 Credit performance outcomes | The under-classification evidence base (reuses H1–H3 cohorts) |
| OPT5 Customer demographic data beyond regulatory minimum | Protected/proxy attributes — **only via a governed fairness-audit process; access requires its own legal review** (source §7.3, verbatim condition) |

## Audit protocol (process level — the analytical protocol itself is agreed with Compliance; detail in `../05-risk-and-compliance/regulatory-workplan.md` §4)
1. **Legal gate first (week 5 decision):** Compliance determines whether protected attributes may be accessed for testing purposes in this jurisdiction, under what safeguards (governed environment, access-logged, purpose-limited, results-only egress). No extraction before written approval.
2. **Metric choice with Compliance (per §8.2):** disparity metrics are chosen with Compliance — not by the analytics team unilaterally — and pre-registered.
3. **Audit logic:** compare under-classification incidence (from H1–H3 cohort results, same ground truth per E2) across protected/proxy groups. Then the direction test: would correcting the evidenced under-classification narrow or widen group disparities?
4. **Blocked path (legitimate per E9):** if legally blocked, document the block formally — reason, what was attempted, residual risk — and obtain executive acceptance of the residual risk. E9 is satisfiable either way; silence is not an option.
5. **Caveats:** proxy attributes carry their own inference risk — the proxy set and its limits are part of the Compliance-agreed protocol; findings are privileged where counsel directs.

## Stakeholder sign-off
- Protocol + metrics: Compliance (owns), Risk consulted. Results: D7 (fairness position, CCO sign-off), D4, D9 (fairness-indicator baselines per §8.2). Executive sign-off required only on the blocked-path residual risk.

## Estimated effort
1.5–2 analyst-weeks under the governed protocol + Compliance effort. Elapsed weeks 5–6 (after the week-5 legal gate).
