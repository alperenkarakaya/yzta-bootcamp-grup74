# Interview Guide — Business / Commercial Team
**Stakeholder profile (source §3):** goals — revenue growth, utilization, cross-sell, competitive offers. Concerns — slow governance killing the initiative; solution too constrained to move numbers. Their success metrics — approved credit volume uplift; utilization; revenue per customer.
**Session:** 90 min (week 2); participants: commercial lead for lending + segment/campaign manager + an RM/branch representative if available.

## Objectives
1. Gather the commercial evidence of the problem (lost customers, exception demand, campaign history — H7, H11, O2).
2. Locate any existing under-lending/foregone-revenue measurement (OQ-05, O5).
3. Test willingness to sign a **risk-adjusted** shared metric (E7) — the make-or-break tension.
4. Calibrate expectations about governance pace and constrained scope (their §3 concern).

## Questions (11)
1. What happened in your last proactive limit-increase campaign — volumes, losses, complaints? *(Appendix B-3, verbatim; H7/U3)*
2. Is there any metric, anywhere, for revenue foregone through conservative offers? *(Appendix B-5, verbatim; O5, pain point §2.3)*
3. Where do relationship managers most often lose sales they sourced — and what happens to those customers? *(§1.3; H11 competitive poaching)*
4. How many exception/upgrade requests does the front line raise, through what process, and how long do they take? *(U7; O2 demand; §2.3 "no mechanism to lift a good customer without full re-underwrite")*
5. Which offer lever matters most commercially — limit, pricing, or product tier — and for which customer groups? *(P3 texture; D5 sizing structure)*
6. If the evidence shows the biggest fixable value is in offer policy (P3) rather than model accuracy (P1), does this initiative still make sense to you? *(tests attachment to the P1 framing; §1.1 critical framing challenge)*
7. Would you sign a success metric that is **risk-adjusted** — where volume uplift that raises expected loss beyond a band counts as failure? What would you need in return? *(E7 core probe, ask verbatim-ish and let the discomfort surface)*
8. Where do you believe Risk is over-conservative, and what evidence do you have beyond anecdote? *(O7; separates evidence from folklore; feeds the tension log)*
9. What utilization patterns tell you an offer was too small? *(U2; H7 interpretation)*
10. What governance turnaround would make this initiative worthwhile vs. dead-on-arrival for you? *(their §3 concern; R-OPS-05; feeds D11 timeline realism)*
11. What would make you call this program a success in 12 months — in one number? *(reveals whether they think risk-adjusted or gross)*

## Evidence to collect
- U3: campaign post-mortems, selection rules, outcome reports (H7's natural experiment).
- U7: exception request logs/volumes/turnaround (O2 demand evidence).
- Sales MI on lost customers/attrition anecdotes → cross-reference U4 (H11).
- Any existing foregone-revenue analysis (OQ-05) — likely absent; absence is itself evidence (§2.3).

## Tensions to probe
- **Risk-vs-Business shared metric (primary):** their instinct is gross volume; E7 requires risk-adjusted. Probe Q7/Q11 hard: will they accept smaller, safer numbers? If not, this stalls at Mid Phase "regardless of technical merit" (§3) — escalate to Sponsor per charter.
- **Constrained-scope disappointment:** the layer will never re-score or auto-approve above policy (§4 boundaries). Do they understand and still want it? A "yes, if it also…" answer is scope-creep risk (R-AI-05) — log it.
- **Patience:** slow governance is their stated concern; test whether pivot options (e.g., O5 measurement-first) keep their sponsorship.

## Feed into D3 (and beyond)
- Position summary, commitments (data pointers for U3/U7), tension-log entries with verbatim quotes on the shared-metric question.
- Direct feeds: H7/H11 context, O2/O5 demand evidence, D9 negotiation starting positions, D5 commercial framing.
