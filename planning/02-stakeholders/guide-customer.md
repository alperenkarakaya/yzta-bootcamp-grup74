# Customer Research Guide
**Stakeholder profile (source §3):** goals — fair offer matching their real profile; transparency; fast decisions. Concerns — opaque decisions; feeling penalized despite good behavior; data privacy ("what are you using about me?"). Their success metrics — higher/right-sized limit; understandable rationale; no new friction.
**Method (per source H9/H10):** small-sample, semi-structured interviews (8–12 customers) + concept testing. Qualitative and directional — never presented as statistics.

## Governance preconditions (before any customer contact)
- Compliance-approved research protocol: recruitment channel, consent wording, incentive policy, data handling (OQ-20).
- No promises: participants must not be led to expect offer changes (responsible-lending and fairness discipline).
- Recruitment cohorts (via governed selection): near-boundary customers, thin-file/new-to-bank, customers with limit-related complaints (from M8), recent decliners of offers — the populations §1.3 names as most impacted.

## Objectives
1. Test A3: is dissatisfaction really about segment/limit outcomes — or communication, speed, opacity?
2. Test H9: explanation/path-to-improvement vs. absolute amount as the driver.
3. Test H10 (customer side): do explanation concepts change acceptance and trust?
4. Capture privacy boundaries: what data use feels acceptable for better offers (A7 social-license texture)?

## Discussion prompts (10 — semi-structured, adapt order to the story)
1. Tell us about the last credit offer or limit decision you received from the bank. What happened, and how did it land with you? *(open frame; A3)*
2. Did you understand why the offer was what it was? What explanation did you get — and what did you want to know? *(H9 explanation gap)*
3. If the amount had been the same but the bank had clearly explained why, and what you could do to improve it — how different would you feel? *(H9's exact discriminator: amount vs. explanation/path)*
4. Have you ever felt the bank's view of you lagged reality — you'd improved, but the offer didn't? What did you do? *(H2/O3 customer texture; H11 switching trigger)*
5. What would make you move this product to another bank? Has a competitor approached you with a better offer? *(H11)*
6. [Show explanation concepts, one at a time — H10] What does this tell you? Would you trust it? Which version would you want? *(comprehension / fairness / trust indicators)*
7. One version mentions the bank "reviewing additional information about how you manage your accounts" to improve offers. How does that sit with you — what's fair use and what's over the line? *(privacy concern verbatim from §3; U1 social license)*
8. If a review found you qualified for a better offer, how would you want to hear about it — and would "our analysis identified you" feel good or creepy? *(O2/O3 delivery texture; headline-risk calibration from the customer seat)*
9. What does a "fair" credit decision mean to you? Have you ever felt penalized despite good behavior? *(§3 fairness perception; H14 lived-experience texture — no protected-attribute questions asked)*
10. If you complained about a limit before: what did you actually want — a bigger number, an explanation, or a path? *(H9 triangulation with M8 taxonomy, complainant sub-sample)*

## Evidence to collect
- Consented recordings/transcripts; coded themes against the H9 taxonomy (same code frame as complaints — triangulation by design).
- Concept-reaction ratings on the pre-agreed qualitative indicators (comprehension / fairness perception / trust direction) → D9 trust baseline.
- Privacy red-line notes (what data use participants reject) → D7 context, D8 legal-basis realism.

## Tensions to probe
- **Amount vs. explanation (the H9 crux):** politeness bias makes people praise explanations; probe with trade-off framing (prompt 3) and complainant recall (prompt 10).
- **Transparency vs. "creepy":** the same data use that improves offers can violate perceived privacy — locate the line, it bounds O1/O8 narratives.
- **AI framing:** neutral-process wording vs. explicit "AI" wording may split trust (H10; executive headline concern) — test both if session time allows.

## Feed into D3 (and beyond)
- Customer-group position summary in D3 (the map's first row, evidenced rather than assumed).
- Direct feeds: H9/H10 results → D4; trust/NPS baselines → D9; O4 value rationale → D5; privacy boundaries → D7/D8.
