# Interview Guide — Customer Service / Front Line
**Stakeholder profile (source §3, "added — brief omitted them"):** goals — fewer complaint escalations; ability to explain outcomes. Concerns — a new layer makes explanations harder ("the AI adjusted it"). Their success metrics — complaint volume on limits; first-contact resolution.
**Why they matter here:** §6.4 mandates "Include front-line staff in stakeholder interviews" (front-line confusion risk); KPI §8.1 requires interview coverage "incl. front line."
**Session:** 60 min group session (week 2) + short shadowing of complaint handling if permitted; participants: branch RMs + contact-center credit-queue agents + a team lead.

## Objectives
1. Ground the complaint reality behind M8: what customers actually say about limits/offers (H9 taxonomy input).
2. Quantify the exception/upgrade request path (U7; O2 demand; §2.3 pain points).
3. Test explanation concepts from the staff side (H10) and the two-layer explainability fear (R-OPS-03).
4. Collect attrition color: what happens when good customers are under-offered (H11 texture).

## Questions (11)
1. When a customer asks "why is my limit this low?", what do you actually say — and what do your tools show you? *(§2.3 "complaints about limits with no explainable answer")*
2. What share of your credit-related contacts are about limit/offer size vs. process speed vs. lack of explanation? *(H9 pre-taxonomy calibration from the people who hear it)*
3. Walk us through the last time you tried to get a good customer a better offer — steps, time, outcome. *(U7; §2.3 "no mechanism to lift a good customer without full re-underwrite"; O2 demand)*
4. How often do such exception requests succeed, and what evidence wins? *(O2's evidence-pack concept realism; H4 override context)*
5. Which customers do you *know* are better than their offer — how do you know, and what happens to them? *(thin-file/boundary anecdotes → H1/H3 color; H11)*
6. Have you seen customers leave for a competitor's better offer — how did you find out? *(H11; U4 destination-data reality check)*
7. If an offer were adjusted by an additional analytical layer, what would you need to explain it confidently at first contact? *(H10 staff-side; R-OPS-03)*
8. Reaction to explanation concepts [show H10 stimuli]: which could you actually use with an upset customer? *(H10 protocol step 3)*
9. Would "our systems reviewed additional information and improved your offer" help or hurt trust — and what wording would you never use? *(§3 concern verbatim: "the AI adjusted it"; feeds H10 and executive headline-risk boundary)*
10. What gets escalated today that shouldn't be — and what can't you resolve that you should? *(first-contact-resolution baseline texture for D9)*
11. If this program only produced better explanations (no offer changes at all), would your job get easier? *(isolates O4 value from O1/O2 value)*

## Evidence to collect
- Access path to complaint logs tagged to credit limits (M8) and their tagging quality (taxonomy feasibility).
- Exception request logs/volumes/turnaround (U7) — or testimony that no such log exists (itself evidence, §2.3).
- Call scripts / knowledge-base articles currently used to explain offers (O4 baseline; D2 step-8 input).
- Anecdote register: boundary/thin-file cases, competitor losses (color for D1, never a substitute for data).

## Tensions to probe
- **Explainability fear:** their stated concern is that the layer makes explanations *harder*. Take it seriously — if H10 staff-side fails, O4/O8 trust value collapses and R-OPS-03 fires.
- **Front line vs. Risk:** do they blame "the system," "Risk," or "policy"? (Feeds the §1.3 Risk-as-growth-blocker tension from the third seat.)
- **Incentive honesty:** are they measured in ways that make them want higher limits regardless of risk (their own asymmetry, mirroring §1.2 asymmetric error costs)?

## Feed into D3 (and beyond)
- Position summary; pain-point evidence with artifacts (logs, scripts); tension-log entries.
- Direct feeds: H9 taxonomy, H10 staff-side results, U7/M8 acquisition paths, D9 baselines (complaint volume, first-contact resolution), D2 step 8 (offer delivery), O2/O4 demand evidence.
