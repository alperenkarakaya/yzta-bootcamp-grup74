# Regulator Dialogue Brief (contingent — not a standard interview)
**Stakeholder profile (source §3, "added"):** external, indirect. Goals — consumer protection, financial stability, model governance. Concerns — AI opacity; discrimination; responsible lending breaches ("over-lending is also a regulatory issue — the fix must not overshoot"). Success metrics — "N/A — but their expectations shape every internal metric."

## Status and preconditions
- The source authorizes only **"(if available) informal regulator dialogue"** as part of H13's validation method. This brief exists so that *if* Compliance judges dialogue wise (their guide, Q11), the conversation is prepared — it is **not** a commitment to engage.
- **Route:** exclusively via Compliance/Legal, within the bank's existing supervisory relationship. The engagement team never contacts a regulator directly. Nothing is "submitted"; no approval is sought or implied.
- **Decision owner:** Chief Compliance Officer decides whether, when, and in what format (OQ-17 jurisdiction context).

## Objectives (if dialogue proceeds)
1. Temperature-check the classification narrative: a layer that optimizes offers strictly **within approved policy ranges**, never re-scoring, never overriding segments, never adjusting engine inputs, never auto-approving above policy (§4 boundaries) — is that credibly "offer optimization," or presumptively "credit decisioning"? (H13/E4)
2. Hear expectations on fairness evidence: would a proactive fairness audit (H14) — including its "correction reduces disparity" framing — be welcomed?
3. Understand explainability expectations for offer-affecting analytics (§6.3 explainability rights; O4 constraints).
4. Learn whether AI-specific regulation applies regardless of internal classification (§6.3, e.g., EU AI Act high-risk treatment — jurisdiction-dependent).

## Themes (framed as positions to test, not questions to fire)
- Draft position from D7-in-progress: classification reasoning + the boundary statement, presented as "our current thinking," inviting reaction.
- The under-lending problem framing: right-sizing (not maximizing) with affordability as a hard constraint — pre-empts the over-lending concern the source attributes to the regulator lens.
- Governance intent: existing human authority for boundary cases (O2), full audit trail, Model Risk classification before any go-live.
- The measurement-first option (O5): analytics that change no offers — a deliberately low-friction anchor for the conversation.

## Evidence to collect
- Compliance's minuted note of the dialogue (tone, cautions, references to guidance) — filed to D7 and the D3 engagement log.
- Any referenced guidance/precedent documents.

## Hard rules
1. No product pitch, no demo, no design talk (none exists in Phase 1 — keep it that way).
2. No commitments on behalf of the bank; no timelines shared beyond "discovery phase."
3. If the informal reading is negative, that is H13 evidence for D7 — it must be reported faithfully, not managed away (charter: outcomes reported faithfully; no-go is a valid outcome).

## Feed into D3 (and beyond)
- Engagement-log entry (dialogue held / declined / not pursued, with reason).
- Direct feeds: D7 classification section, E4 confidence, D11 conditions.
