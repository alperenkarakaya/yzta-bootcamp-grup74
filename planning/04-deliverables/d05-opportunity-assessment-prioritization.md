# D5 — Opportunity Assessment & Prioritization (template)
**Source §9:** "sized, risk-adjusted, ranked O1–O8; recommended MVP candidate scope (scope only — not design)" · **Owner:** Product + Finance · **Sign-off:** Executives · **Feeds gates:** E3 (primary), E1 (value-floor branch), consumes E4 viability flags

> Guidance: the §4 sizing rule is the method — "size each opportunity (population touched × plausible value per customer × risk cost) using bank data, and rank. The MVP candidate list for Mid Phase should come from this ranking — not from what is technically most interesting." Everything here is risk-adjusted from day one (R-BUS-01 mitigation); gross numbers never appear without net counterparts.

## 1. Executive summary and recommendation
> Guidance: ranking headline, net value ranges, and the recommendation — which may be **proceed on a candidate scope, pivot (e.g., offer-policy fix or measurement-only O5), or stop**. "No-go is a valid outcome" is binding here: if no opportunity clears the agreed value floor (E1/OQ-13) net of risk, this document recommends stop or pivot, and that is a successful discovery outcome (§10).

## 2. Method and constraints
> Guidance: sizing formula per §4 (population × value per customer × risk cost), risk-adjustment per the H6 Finance+Risk method; constraints applied as hard bounds: risk appetite headroom (A5/M7), affordability (§6.1 overshoot mitigation, §6.3 responsible lending), legal viability flags per opportunity (from D7/H13), within-policy-range operation only (§4 boundary).

## 3. Opportunity sizing sheets O1–O8
> Guidance: one subsection per opportunity (O1 within-segment differentiation · O2 boundary-case flagging · O3 timing/refresh · O4 explanation layer · O5 under-lending measurement · O6 graduated offers · O7 counter-conservatism input · O8 advisory enrichment). Each: population touched (evidence: which H results), plausible value per customer (range), risk cost, net risk-adjusted range, downside scenario, governance friction (carry the §4 low→high ratings), legal viability flag (E4), evidence dependencies (which H supports/undermines it).
> Required evidence: H5/H7 → O1; H1/H4 + U7 → O2; H2/H8 → O3; H9/H10 → O4; H6/M9 → O5; H7 → O6; H1/H2/H6 → O7; H5/H12 → O8.

## 4. Per-segment win-win test (A10)
> Guidance: A10 verbatim challenge — "'win-win' must be proven per segment, not asserted." Show, per segment, both the customer-outcome and portfolio-risk directions. Segments failing the test are excluded from sized value, listed, not smoothed.

## 5. Downside scenario (E3 requirement)
> Guidance: E3's test requires "net value range documented, including downside scenario." State the downside assumptions (loss deterioration, appetite tightening, adoption shortfall) and the resulting floor value.

## 6. Ranking and MVP candidate scope
> Guidance: ranked table with rationale; the MVP candidate list is **scope only — not design** (source verbatim). Scope statements name the opportunity, target populations, and the boundaries carried from §4 ("the layer shall never…"), nothing about how it would be built.

## 7. Sensitivities and honesty box
> Guidance: which single evidence reversals would change the ranking; claims resting on "scheduled" hypotheses; counterfactual limits (§2.4). Written for a steering committee that must not be oversold (R-BUS-05).
