> Guidance: production plan for this paper is `../05-risk-and-compliance/regulatory-workplan.md`. This paper states positions and conditions — it does not design controls or systems.

## 1. Questions presented
> Guidance: (a) can the bridge layer be classified as "offer optimization within approved policy" rather than "credit decisioning" (H13)? (b) which opportunities O1–O8 are viable under which classification? (c) what is the fair-lending position (H14)? (d) what legal bases cover the data used?

## 2. Factual basis
> Guidance: the concept as opined on — strictly the boundary statement from charter §2 (the §4 "shall never" list quoted verbatim) and within-policy-range operation. If the opined-on concept drifts from these boundaries, the opinion is void — state this expressly (R-AI-05 guard).

## 3. Classification opinion (H13 / E4)
> Required evidence: written legal reasoning; precedent review (internal overlay classifications; supervisory precedent where public); conditions under which the classification holds; conditions that would flip it. E4's test: the opinion "confirms at least one opportunity (O1–O8) can proceed under an acceptable classification" — include the per-opportunity viability table consumed by D5. Falsification (H13) reported plainly if concluded: "the layer is a credit decision model" → E4 consequence: "Restrict scope to compliant opportunities or stop."

## 4. Jurisdiction scan
> Required evidence: per jurisdiction (OQ-17): consumer-credit rules touching offer differentiation; model-governance expectations; data protection / purpose limitation; explainability rights (§6.3); responsible-lending/affordability rules; AI-specific regulation (§6.3 names the EU AI Act's high-risk treatment of creditworthiness AI as the exemplar — applicability concluded, not assumed).

## 5. Fair-lending position (H14 / E9)
> Guidance: either (a) audit executed — protocol reference, Compliance-chosen disparity metrics (§8.2), findings, and the direction test (would correction narrow or widen disparity — the "compliance remediation" reframing if it narrows, per §5 note); or (b) audit formally blocked — documented reason, what was attempted, residual risk statement, and executive acceptance record. E9 admits both paths; silence admits neither.

## 6. Data legal-basis map
> Required evidence: legal basis per collected data category (tracker items M1–M9, U1–U8, OPT2/3/5 as applicable), purpose-limitation analysis for repurposed data (U1), bureau-license findings (U6/OQ-24), consent-gap register with remediation stance (§6.5). This section co-signs D8's legal-basis column.

## 7. Conditions and guardrails for any Mid Phase
