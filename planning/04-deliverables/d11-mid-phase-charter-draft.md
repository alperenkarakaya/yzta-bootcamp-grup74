# D11 — Mid Phase Charter, Draft (template)
**Source §9:** "scope, boundaries ('the layer shall never…'), timeline, resource ask, go/no-go recommendation" · **Owner:** Product · **Sign-off:** Steering committee · **Feeds gates:** E10 (the decision artifact)

> Guidance: this draft is written to enable a clean steering decision — it must make **stop and pivot as decidable as proceed**. Per the §10 charter principle, a phase that only ever recommends proceeding has failed as a discovery phase. Prepare the recommendation section honestly; the steering committee, not the drafting team, decides (E10).

## 1. Recommendation
> Guidance: exactly one of — **Proceed** (to Mid Phase on the scope below) / **Pivot** (named alternative: e.g., offer-policy recalibration via O7 route, or measurement-only O5 scope, or "the model is fine, invest in offer policy instead" per §10) / **Stop** (with preserved-value statement). State the evidence chain: gate statuses E1–E9, the P1/P2/P3 mix (D1), and the D5 ranking that produced this recommendation.

## 2. Scope (if proceed or pivot)
> Guidance: the MVP candidate scope from D5 §6 — opportunity(ies), target populations, value hypothesis carried. **Scope only — not design** (source D5 wording binds here too). Anything requiring solution design is listed as Mid Phase work, not resolved here.

## 3. Boundaries — "the layer shall never…"
> Guidance: quote §4 verbatim: the layer shall never re-score customers, override segments automatically, adjust the engine's inputs, or auto-approve above-policy limits — "Any of these would make the layer a de facto credit decision model and trigger the full regulatory weight the concept is designed to avoid." Add conditions attached by D7 (legal) and D10 (governance) as charter-level constraints. These boundaries are inherited, not renegotiable in Mid Phase without returning to the steering committee.

## 4. Timeline and stage gates
> Guidance: Mid Phase timeline aligned to the bank's *real* approval windows (§6.4 change-management mitigation; OQ-10 evidence), with stage gates that preserve the no-go principle — every Mid Phase gate keeps stop as an outcome.

## 5. Resource ask
> Guidance: roles, capacity, data-environment needs, external counsel/validation budget — grounded in the D5 scope and D10 governance expectations. Ranges, not precision theater.

## 6. Success metrics carried forward
> Guidance: the signed D9 book governs; restate the shared metric, tolerance band, and baseline values. No new metrics may be introduced without the joint Risk+Business signature that E7 established.

## 7. Risks carried forward
> Guidance: D6 §3 Phase-2 mitigations table, referenced not duplicated; the feedback-loop design requirement (R-AI-02, §6.2) called out explicitly as a Mid Phase obligation.

## 8. Conditions precedent
> Guidance: anything E-gates attached (E4 legal conditions, E5 approved workarounds, E8 governance conditions, E9 residual-risk acceptance) — listed as conditions that must hold at Mid Phase start.

## 9. Decision record (E10)
> Guidance: completed at the steering committee using the minuted-decision template in `../06-gates/exit-criteria-checklist.md` §E10 — decision, rationale, dissents, conditions, signatures. "Phase is not closed until a decision is minuted."
