# Exit Criteria Gate Checklist — E1–E10
**Source §10, applied as gates:** "These are gates, not checkboxes; failing a gate has a defined consequence." Objective tests and consequences below are the source's, verbatim. Status is updated weekly from week 6; final judgment at the week-10 steering committee.
**Standing principle (source §10, quoted):** "the Early Phase is successful if it produces a *correct* decision — including 'the model is fine, invest in offer policy instead' or 'stop.' A phase that only ever recommends proceeding has failed as a discovery phase."

## Gate operating rules
1. **No gate is judged by the team that produced its evidence alone** — the gate-judge column is the sign-off authority from source §9/§10.
2. **Thresholds are pre-registered** (E1 values via OQ-13, E7 band via D9 negotiation) before evidence lands — moving a threshold after results are known is an R-BUS-05 trigger and goes to the Sponsor.
3. **A failed gate executes its consequence** — it does not get argued into a pass. Consequences are the source's own and include stop, pivot, extension, and escalation paths.
4. **Partial evidence** is reported as TODO/AT-RISK at the weekly working group from week 6, so week 9's buffer is spent on real shortfalls.

## E10 — Minuted Go/No-Go Decision (template)

> To be completed in the steering committee session (week 10). The decision is not valid until every field is filled and signed. Options are equal-status by charter: **a stop or pivot decision is a successful phase outcome** (§10).

**Meeting:** date / location / chair
**Attendees & quorum:** (steering committee members present; quorum per charter §4 — joint Risk–Business representation required)
**Pack of record:** steering pack version + D1–D11 statuses attached

**1. Gate review record** — for each of E1–E9: met / not met / met-with-conditions (list conditions).

**2. Options considered (all three must be minuted with their case, even the ones not chosen):**
- **PROCEED** to Mid Phase on D11 scope — evidence for; conditions precedent (D11 §8).
- **PIVOT** — named alternative scope (e.g., offer-policy recalibration via O7, measurement-only O5, or "the model is fine, invest in offer policy instead" per §10); what evidence drives the pivot.
- **STOP** — evidence for; preserved value statement (what the bank keeps: D1 diagnosis, D9 baselines, O5 measurement case, D2 map, D7 position).

**3. DECISION:** ☐ PROCEED ☐ PIVOT (scope: ______) ☐ STOP

**4. Rationale (minuted, mandatory):** the evidence chain the committee relied on — gates, D1 mix, D5 ranking, risk positions.

**5. Dissents and abstentions:** recorded by name with reasons (a silent dissent is a governance defect).

**6. Conditions attached to the decision:** owner + due date each.

**7. Follow-up obligations:** communication plan (incl. front line), asset archival/handover, register handover (risks, open questions).
